Online Scams

Online scams are fraudulent activities done over the internet where scammers trick people into sharing money, passwords, or personal information. They usually come through fake emails, messages, websites, calls, or social media, pretending to be trusted sources like banks or companies. The goal is to cheat users for financial gain or identity theft, often by creating fear, urgency, or false offers. üö®

Types of Online Scams

Phishing Scams

Scammers send fake emails or messages pretending to be trusted sources. They try to trick you into revealing passwords, bank details, or personal information. They may ask for OTPs or other verification codes to gain access to your accounts. If you share these, your money, accounts, or sensitive data can be stolen without warning.

Online Shopping Scams

Fake websites or sellers offer products at very low prices to attract buyers. They often ask for your card details, personal information, or payment upfront. Once payment is made, the product is never delivered. Victims lose money and sometimes sensitive information without any way to recover it.

Lottery & Prize Scams

You may receive messages claiming that you have won a lottery or prize. Scammers try to create excitement and urgency to make you act quickly. They ask for fees, bank details, or personal information to release the prize. In reality, there is no prize, and your money or data gets stolen.

Tech Support Scams

Fraudsters pretend to be official tech support from well-known companies. They contact you claiming there is a problem with your device or account. They ask you to give remote access or pay for fake services. Once they have access, they steal sensitive information or money from you.

Be Aware of This Scam

  • Always verify the sender before opening emails, messages, or links.
  • Never share personal information, passwords, or OTPs with strangers.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading unknown attachments.
  • Check website URLs carefully before making online purchases.
  • Be cautious of deals or prizes that seem too good to be true.
  • Keep your devices and software updated with the latest security patches.
  • Report any suspicious activity or scams to authorities or platforms.
  • Educate yourself and others about common online scam tactics.
