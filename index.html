What is SCAM?


A scam is a dishonest act used to cheat people for money or personal information. Scammers often pretend to be trusted individuals or organizations. They use fear, urgency, or fake offers to manipulate victims. Being aware of scams helps people stay safe and protect themselves online.


Recognizing Scam Victims

Digital scams and Cyber Awareness


Scams are increasing rapidly in both online and offline spaces. They can happen through calls, messages, emails, or fake websites. Many people lose money or personal information due to scams. Scammers use social media, fake offers, and phishing emails to trick individuals. Young and elderly people are often the most vulnerable targets. Lack of awareness or ignoring warning signs makes people fall victim. To stay safe, always verify sources before clicking links or responding. Never share OTPs, passwords, or personal details with anyone. Reporting scams to authorities and educating others can prevent losses. Being cautious and spreading awareness helps build a safer community.

Types of Scams

Online Scams

An online scamis a type of internet fraud where scammers pretend to be trustworthy to deceive people. They trick victims using fake emails, messages, websites, or social media to steal personal, financial, or banking information. The main goal is to take money or misuse sensitive data for illegal purposes.

Phone&Message Scams

Phone/Message Scams are fraudulent attempts to steal personal or financial information using calls or text messages. Scammers often pose as banks, officials, or companies to trick victims into revealing sensitive data. These scams can lead to identity theft, loss, or unauthorized access if precautions are not taken.

Financial Scams

Financial scams trick people into giving money or sensitive information. They include credit card fraud, fake loans, Ponzi schemes, and fake charities. Victims often lose money or have their accounts compromised. These scams can happen online or offline, targeting anyone.

Identity Scams

Identity scams occur when fraudsters steal personal information to commit fraud. They may open accounts, take loans, or access existing online accounts in someone else‚Äôs name. Victims can suffer financial loss or damage to their credit. Protecting personal information is key to avoiding these scams.

Offline Scams

Offline scams involve real-world interactions where fraudsters trick victims. Examples include door-to-door scams, fake job offers, or lottery/prize schemes. Victims may lose money or share sensitive information unknowingly. Staying cautious and verifying offers helps prevent such scams.

Modern Scams

Specialized scams use modern tools or platforms to defraud people. Examples business email compromise, and fake rental listings. These are often sophisticated and can result in large financial losses. Awareness and careful verification are essential to stay safe.
